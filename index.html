About me

I am a 3rd-year Computer Engineering student at Texas Tech University, where I've developed a strong foundation in software development, embedded system design, robotics, and artificial intelligence. Currently, I'm working on my junior project lab focused on developing a point to point infrared communication system, under the guidance of Everett Mcarthur.

During my academic career, I've maintained a 3.3 GPA while gaining hands-on experience in embedded systems, data structures, and mathematical computing. I've also served as a robotics mentor for the university, collaborating with the Get Excited About Robotics (GEAR) organization to help students grow their interest in STEM fields through lego robotics, while challenging them to think outside the box in their approach to automation and system optimization.

My technical interests center around machine learning, embedded devices, robotics, and computer vision. I recently completed a project working on Gesture controlled LEDS, where I Implemented a python-based computer vision program to identify the number of finger-like contours in a predefined area of interest and make requests to a local HTML web server hosted on an ESP12 to control LEDs dynamically based on user gestures. I'm particularly passionate about cutting-edge technologies that integrate AI and robotics to solve real-world problems, such as automation, sustainable technologies, and healthcare advancements.

Beyond academics, I'm an avid electronics hobbyist and am perpetually working on value driven projects to help optimize my personal life. I also enjoy giving back to the wider engineering community by serving as a Launch Your Future in Engineering (LYFE) peer mentor. When I'm not tinkering, you can find me exploring new vintage clothing shops or training Mui-tai and Brazilian-Jiu-Jitsu at our university's martial arts club.
